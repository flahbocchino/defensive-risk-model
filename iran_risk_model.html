01
The Master Formula
How the final risk score is calculated from four independent dimensions

The model uses a multiplicative risk formula, meaning all four factors amplify each other. A location with moderate scores on all factors can still reach a high final score — just as a location very strong on one factor but weak on others stays contained. This reflects real-world risk: a target that is highly exposed but well-defended is less dangerous than one moderately exposed and completely unprotected.

Rn = ( A × E × V × I ) ÷ 625 × 100

Each variable is scored 1–5. The maximum raw product is 5⁴ = 625, which we divide by to normalize the result into a 0–100 scale.

A
Threat (Ameaça)
Scale: 1 (negligible) → 5 (confirmed pattern)
E
Exposure
Scale: 1 (isolated) → 5 (fully exposed)
V
Vulnerability
Scale: 1 (hardened) → 5 (unprotected)
I
Impact
Scale: 1 (negligible) → 5 (catastrophic)

Why multiply and not add? Addition treats factors as independent — a score of 5+1+1+1 = 8 would look similar to 2+2+2+2 = 8. But in real risk, a target with maximum threat and zero vulnerability is very different from one with moderate levels across all four. Multiplication captures interdependence: the formula punishes combinations where multiple factors are simultaneously high.

02
Factor-by-Factor Calculation
Each of the four main variables is itself a weighted sub-formula

Each of the four primary factors (A, E, V, I) is not a single estimate — it is calculated from multiple sub-variables with assigned weights. The weights reflect how much each sub-variable contributes to the final factor score. They always sum to 1.0 (100%).

A
Threat — measuring attack probability and historical intent

The Threat score answers: How likely is this location to be targeted based on past behavior and current context? It combines four sub-variables:

A = (0.35 × P) + (0.25 × H) + (0.20 × S) + (0.20 × T)
P — Strategic Priority
How valuable is this target to an attacker? Military base, energy hub, symbolic landmark.
Weight: 35% · Scale: 1–5
H — Historical Pattern
Has this type of target been struck before? Documented precedent increases weight significantly.
Weight: 25% · Scale: 1–5
S — Signaling / Escalation
Current public statements, alerts, mobilization, rhetoric. Higher = more explicit threat.
Weight: 20% · Scale: 1–5
T — Regional Tension
Overall conflict temperature in the surrounding region. Serves as a baseline amplifier.
Weight: 20% · Scale: 1–5
Example (UAE energy infrastructure, March 2026): A = (0.35×5) + (0.25×5) + (0.20×4) + (0.20×5) = 4.80
E
Exposure — how reachable and identifiable is this location

Exposure measures how geographically and operationally "in the open" a location is. A hardened base in a remote area may have low exposure; a major port on a well-known coastline has very high exposure.

E = (0.40 × G) + (0.30 × D) + (0.20 × C) + (0.10 × O)
G — Geography / Position
Is the location within missile or drone range? On a coastline? Near a conflict corridor?
Weight: 40% · Scale: 1–5
D — Asset Density
Density of high-value assets nearby. Clustered assets raise collective exposure.
Weight: 30% · Scale: 1–5
C — Logistical Connectivity
Connected to key ports, roads, pipelines, airports — interdependence multiplies exposure.
Weight: 20% · Scale: 1–5
O — Observability
Can the location be easily identified via satellite, public data, or OSINT? Known locations are easier to target.
Weight: 10% · Scale: 1–5
Example (UAE energy infrastructure, March 2026): E = (0.40×5) + (0.30×4) + (0.20×5) + (0.10×5) = 4.70
V
Vulnerability — how protected is the location against an attack

Important: for Vulnerability, a higher score means weaker protection. A score of 5 means the location has no physical hardening, no fast response capability, no backup systems. A score of 1 means it is well-defended and resilient.

V = (0.30 × F) + (0.25 × R) + (0.20 × B) + (0.15 × C) + (0.10 × M)
F — Physical Fragility
Structural hardening of buildings and infrastructure. Exposed pipelines, open-air equipment = high score.
Weight: 30% · Scale: 1–5
R — Response Capability
Speed and effectiveness of emergency, military, and medical response. Slower = more vulnerable.
Weight: 25% · Scale: 1–5
B — Passive Protection
Shelters, blast barriers, dispersion of personnel, redundant access routes.
Weight: 20% · Scale: 1–5
C — Operational Continuity
Can operations continue after an attack? Redundant power, communications, command systems.
Weight: 15% · Scale: 1–5
M — Monitoring
Early warning systems, surveillance coverage, threat detection capability. Gaps increase vulnerability.
Weight: 10% · Scale: 1–5
Example (UAE energy infrastructure, March 2026): V = (0.30×3) + (0.25×3) + (0.20×3) + (0.15×4) + (0.10×2) = 3.05
I
Impact — consequences if an attack actually succeeds

Impact measures the downstream consequences of a successful strike. Even a location with moderate threat and low vulnerability may warrant high attention if the impact of a single successful hit would be catastrophic.

I = (0.35 × O) + (0.25 × H) + (0.20 × E) + (0.20 × S)
O — Operational Impact
Disruption to mission-critical operations. Airport closure, refinery shutdown, base destruction.
Weight: 35% · Scale: 1–5
H — Human Impact
Potential casualties — personnel and civilian populations. Triggers automatic alert if score = 5.
Weight: 25% · Scale: 1–5
E — Economic Impact
Financial damage, supply chain disruption, market impact. Energy infrastructure scores highest here.
Weight: 20% · Scale: 1–5
S — Symbolic / Political Impact
Psychological and political significance. Embassies, landmarks, command centers score high on this axis.
Weight: 20% · Scale: 1–5
Example (UAE energy infrastructure, March 2026): I = (0.35×5) + (0.25×4) + (0.20×5) + (0.20×4) = 4.55
Final Score Calculation · UAE Energy Infrastructure Example
A (Threat) = 4.80   E (Exposure) = 4.70   V (Vulnerability) = 3.05   I (Impact) = 4.55
Raw score = 4.80 × 4.70 × 3.05 × 4.55 = 313.4
Normalized = (313.4 ÷ 625) × 100 = 50.1 → RELEVANT / approaching HIGH

07
Automatic Alert Triggers
Rules that activate alerts independent of the total score

The final score alone is not always sufficient. Some individual sub-variable values should trigger an automatic protective response regardless of how the other factors score. These are called hard triggers. Additionally, the trend of the score over time matters as much as its absolute value.

!
Hard triggers (immediate yellow alert)
These single-variable conditions activate alert status regardless of total score:
Human Impact (H) = 5 → risk to life confirmed
Strategic Priority (P) = 5 → maximum target value
Physical Fragility (F) = 5 → unprotected asset
Signaling (S) = 5 → explicit public threat declared
Δ
Trend monitoring (daily delta)
Track how the score changes over time using these formulas:
ΔR = R(today) − R(yesterday) → daily change
MM3 = (R1 + R2 + R3) ÷ 3 → 3-day moving average
If ΔR > +10 in 24h → yellow alert regardless of total
If MM3 rises for 3 consecutive days → posture review
